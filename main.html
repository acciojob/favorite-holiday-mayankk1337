Bali


Bali is a province of Indonesia and the westernmost of the Lesser Sunda Islands. East of Java and west of Lombok, the province includes the island of Bali and a few smaller offshore islands, notably Nusa Penida, Nusa Lembongan, and Nusa Ceningan to the southeast

  1. Uluwatu Temple-dramatic clifftop sea views and Kecak dance at sunset.
  2. Tegallalang Rice Terrace— iconic emerald paddies with jungle swings.
  3. Ubud Monkey Forest— sacred sanctuary of grey macaques and ancient temples.
  4. Tanah Lot— oceanfront temple famous for itsgolden-hour silhouette.
  5. Mount Batur— pre-dawn hike for an unforgettable sunrise over the caldera.
  • Babi Guling— Bali’s famous roast suckling pig, often enjoyed at ceremonial feast.
  • Bebek Betutu— slow-cooked duck with spices wrapped in banana leaves.
  • Nasi Campur— a plate of rice with small portions of meat, vegetables, peanuts, eggs and sambal.
  • Sate Lilit— minced meat satay wrapped around lemongrass sticks, full of Balinese flavor.
  • Lawar— a traditional mix of vegetables, coconut, and minced meat with spices.
